# Ansi Fortress: The Impregnable Defense of Goguryeo

Ansi Fortress (安市城) stands as one of the most legendary defensive victories in East Asian military history. In 645 CE, this Goguryeo fortress withstood a 3-month siege by Tang Emperor Taizong's massive invasion force, ultimately forcing the withdrawal of one of China's greatest military campaigns.

## Historical Context

In 645 CE, Tang Emperor Taizong (Li Shimin) launched a massive invasion of Goguryeo with over 150,000 troops, intending to conquer the Korean kingdom and expand Tang's territory into the Korean Peninsula. The campaign targeted key Goguryeo fortresses in the Liaodong region, with Ansi Fortress becoming the decisive battleground.

## The Siege of Ansi Fortress (645 CE)

### The Defenders
- **Commander**: Yang Manchun (양만춘)
- **Garrison**: Estimated 5,000-10,000 Goguryeo soldiers
- **Strategy**: Hold the fortress at all costs, deny Tang forces a foothold, delay until winter

### The Attackers
- **Commander**: Emperor Taizong of Tang (Li Shimin, 李世民)
- **Forces**: 150,000+ elite Tang troops
- **Siege Equipment**: Advanced siege towers, battering rams, catapults
- **Objective**: Capture Ansi Fortress to open the route to Pyongyang

### The 3-Month Battle

The siege lasted over 60 days of intense combat:

1. **Initial Assault**: Tang forces launched massive attacks using siege equipment
2. **Goguryeo Countermeasures**: Defenders repaired walls at night, conducted sorties to destroy siege weapons
3. **Siege Mound**: Tang forces built a massive earthen mound to overlook the fortress walls
4. **Mound Capture**: Goguryeo forces captured the mound in a daring night raid
5. **Prolonged Stalemate**: Despite overwhelming numbers, Tang forces could not breach the defenses
6. **Strategic Withdrawal**: With winter approaching, supply lines stretched, and mounting casualties, Emperor Taizong ordered retreat

### The Outcome

**Goguryeo Victory**: The successful defense of Ansi Fortress:
- Forced Emperor Taizong to abandon his invasion
- Saved Goguryeo from conquest
- Became a symbol of Korean resistance and military brilliance
- Demonstrated that superior numbers don't guarantee victory

## The Logic of Ansi's Defense

### 1. Terrain Advantage
Ansi Fortress was built on elevated, mountainous terrain with:
- Natural cliffs protecting multiple sides
- Clear lines of sight for early warning
- Difficult approach routes for siege equipment
- High ground advantage for defenders

### 2. Fortification Design
The fortress featured:
- Multiple concentric walls
- Internal water sources for siege resistance
- Defensive towers at key points
- Reinforced gates with kill zones
- Storage facilities for long-term supplies

### 3. Military Leadership
Commander Yang Manchun demonstrated:
- Exceptional tactical skill in defending against superior numbers
- High morale maintenance under extreme pressure
- Intelligent use of nighttime operations
- Effective coordination of limited forces

### 4. Strategic Patience
The defenders understood that:
- Time was their ally (winter would force Tang retreat)
- Each day of resistance weakened enemy morale and supplies
- A defensive victory didn't require destroying the enemy, only denying their objective

## Modern Military Parallels

### Asymmetric Defense
Like modern asymmetric warfare, Ansi Fortress showed how:
- **Smaller forces** can defeat larger ones through strategic defense
- **Fortifications** multiply defensive effectiveness
- **Local knowledge** of terrain provides crucial advantages
- **Morale and determination** can overcome material superiority

### Resource Management
The siege demonstrates principles still relevant today:
- **Supply chain security** is often more critical than tactical superiority
- **Resource optimization** during prolonged conflicts
- **Environmental factors** (weather, terrain) as strategic weapons

### Leadership in Crisis
Yang Manchun's defense exemplifies:
- **Calm under pressure**: Maintaining discipline during 60+ days of assault
- **Adaptive tactics**: Responding to Tang siege innovations
- **Morale maintenance**: Keeping troops motivated despite overwhelming odds
- **Strategic thinking**: Understanding the broader context (winter, Tang logistics)

## Historical Significance

The defense of Ansi Fortress:
- **Saved Goguryeo**: Prevented Tang conquest of the Korean Peninsula
- **Changed East Asian history**: Maintained Goguryeo as a regional power for another 23 years
- **Humbled a great emperor**: Forced Emperor Taizong's only major military failure
- **Inspired resistance**: Became a symbol of Korean military excellence and national pride

## Legacy

Ansi Fortress remains a powerful example of how:
- **Strategic positioning** can overcome numerical inferiority
- **Defensive architecture** multiplies military effectiveness
- **Leadership and morale** are often more decisive than material advantages
- **Patience and timing** are weapons in defensive warfare

The fortress stands as a testament to the principle that security comes not from overwhelming force, but from intelligent design, determined leadership, and strategic use of advantages.

**Note**: The exact location of Ansi Fortress is disputed among historians, with several sites in the Liaodong region proposed as candidates. However, the historical significance of the siege remains undisputed in Korean, Chinese, and world military history.
